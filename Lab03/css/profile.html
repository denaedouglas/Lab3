This is D'Angelo (Michael Eugene Archer), my favorite
musician/artist. He began his mainstream music career
in 1995 with the critically acclaimed album, Brown Sugar.
As a master musician and distinguished songwriter, he made
his mark on the music scene, and paved the way for many artists
in the genres like R&B/Soul as well as neo-soul. Five years later
he released the platinum selling album Voodoo (2000). He shaped
his own style and carved out a real place for himself with the
album's deep lyrics, raw beats, and beautiful melodies. After a 14
year hiatus, D'Angelo made his return to the music scene when he
released Black Messiah (2014), a politically charged album with a
message for the times regarding the senseless killings of young black
men by police officers. In addition to the thought provoking lyrics of
songs like The Charade, and Prayer, D'Angelo doesn't fall short in
the groove department. After 20+ years on the music scene, D'Angelo
is as fresh and relevant as ever.
